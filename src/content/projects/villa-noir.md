description: >
  Villa Noir sits on a narrow urban plot in Kochi, and the brief was simple:
  give a family of

  four privacy from the street without losing daylight. The response is a house that turns

  inward - a series of courtyards and light wells thread air and sun through the plan while a

  near-blind street facade holds the noise of the city at bay.


  Board-marked concrete, teak screens, and a restrained material palette let the architecture

  recede so daily life can take the foreground. Every room opens onto green - a planted

  threshold, a courtyard, or the rear garden - so the house never feels sealed despite its

  guarded street presence.
